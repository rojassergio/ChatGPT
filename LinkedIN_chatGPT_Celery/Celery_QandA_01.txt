THE TASK: write what you know about celery
***THE ANSWER***      Celery is a versatile vegetable that belongs to the Apiaceae family. It is known for its crisp texture and mild, slightly bitter taste. Celery is commonly used in cooking and is a staple ingredient in many dishes, including soups, stews, salads, and stir-fries.

One of the notable characteristics of celery is its high water content, which makes it a hydrating and low-calorie food choice. It is also a good source of dietary fiber, vitamins, and minerals. Celery is particularly rich in vitamin K, vitamin C, potassium, and folate.

In addition to its nutritional value, celery has been associated with several health benefits. It is believed to have anti-inflammatory properties and may help reduce the risk of chronic diseases like heart disease and cancer. Celery is also known for its potential to aid digestion, promote weight loss, and support overall gut health.

Furthermore, celery seeds and celery seed extract are used in traditional medicine for their potential diuretic, anti-inflammatory, and antioxidant effects. These seeds are often used as a spice or in herbal supplements.

Overall, celery is a versatile and nutritious vegetable that can be enjoyed in various ways, offering both culinary and potential health benefits.======
THE TASK: summarize what you know about celery negative effects on health
***THE ANSWER***      Celery is generally considered a healthy vegetable due to its low calorie content and high fiber and water content. However, there are a few potential negative effects associated with celery consumption.

One of the main concerns is that celery contains a compound called psoralen, which can make the skin more sensitive to sunlight. This can lead to an increased risk of sunburn or skin irritation, especially in individuals with photosensitivity or those who consume large amounts of celery.

Additionally, celery is known to contain natural chemicals called oxalates, which can contribute to the formation of kidney stones in susceptible individuals. People with a history of kidney stones or those prone to developing them should moderate their celery intake.

Some individuals may also experience digestive issues after consuming celery, such as bloating, gas, or diarrhea. This is mainly due to the high fiber content of celery, which can be difficult to digest for some people.

It's important to note that these negative effects are relatively rare and usually occur when celery is consumed in excessive amounts. As with any food, moderation is key to enjoying the health benefits of celery while minimizing any potential drawbacks.======
